# Cinematography & Camera Control Guide

This guide explains how to use the advanced camera control system in Director AI to create professional-grade cinematic visuals.

## Overview
The system automatically adapts the camera behavior based on the **Emotion** and **Action** of the scene.

## Key Camera Techniques
- **Dynamic Framing:** Uses the Rule of Thirds and Leading Lines to guide the viewer's eye.
- **Rack Focus:** Automatically shifts focus to the character's eyes or objects of interest to enhance emotional depth.
- **Handheld Shake:** Adds a subtle, realistic shake during intense action or high-tension scenes to increase immersion.
- **Angle Psychology:**
    - *Low Angle:* Makes the character look powerful and heroic.
    - *Dutch Tilt:* Used for scenes with high tension, confusion, or psychological stress.
    - *Close-up:* Focuses on micro-expressions and lip-sync during dialogue.

## How to Customize
You can influence the camera behavior by changing the `emotion` or `action` in your scene configuration. For example:
- Setting emotion to **"Intense"** will trigger handheld camera movements and tighter framing.
- Setting emotion to **"Calm"** will trigger smooth, slow tracking shots with wide lenses.

## Technical Specs
- **Lens:** Default 35mm cinematic lens.
- **Depth of Field:** Shallow (blurred background) to emphasize the subject.
- **Resolution Target:** 4K Cinematic.
